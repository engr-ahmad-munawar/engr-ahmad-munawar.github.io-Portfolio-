## 🚀 Introduction

University life often begins with excitement, but for me, it started with confusion, unexpected decisions, and family pressure. My admission journey at UET was not straightforward — it involved changing disciplines, unexpected merit lists, and tough choices. Through this experience, I learned about adaptability, patience, and handling unexpected turns in life.

---

## 1️⃣ First Merit List: Mathematics vs Electrical

When the first UET merit list appeared on **31st July**, I was excited to see my name. I had applied for **Electrical Engineering**, but surprisingly, my name appeared in the **Mathematics department**. Initially, I was confused, because my real interest was in Electrical.  

Someone advised me that if I submitted fees now, my name might drop from the current discipline, and I could secure a chance in Electrical in the next merit list. Trusting this advice, I went ahead with the payment. It felt like a risky move at the time, but I hoped it would eventually place me in the department I truly wanted. This first step taught me that sometimes decisions have to be made with limited information and a lot of trust.

---

## 2️⃣ Next Merit List: Electrical Engineering

The second merit list brought relief — my name finally appeared in **Electrical Engineering**, which was my preferred discipline. I was overjoyed and felt like things were finally aligning with my goals.  

However, during this time, I realized I didn’t know much about **downgrading policies** or how discipline changes worked at UET. While everyone else seemed to be making smooth transitions, I had gaps in understanding the process. This confusion made me cautious, but it also reminded me of the importance of paying attention to procedural details in big institutions.

---

## 3️⃣ Family Pressure and the Computer Engineering Switch

Despite my success in getting Electrical, my family had other ideas. They strongly suggested that I should go for **Computer Engineering** instead. At first, I was reluctant — Electrical had been my dream, and I was already seeing my name there. But with constant encouragement (and a little pressure) from my family, I agreed to enroll in Computer Engineering.  

At the time, it felt like I was letting go of my personal choice, but in hindsight, it was a lesson in **adaptability** and **trusting guidance from those who care about you**. Sometimes, life doesn’t follow the exact path you imagined, and learning to adjust gracefully is just as important as pursuing your own plans.

---

## 🎯 Conclusion

Looking back, my UET admission journey was full of unexpected turns: appearing in Mathematics first, securing Electrical in the next merit list, and finally joining Computer Engineering due to family advice. It taught me that **life is not always linear**, and handling confusion, following advice wisely, and adapting to circumstances are vital skills.  

While I may not have ended up exactly where I initially wanted, the experience strengthened my problem-solving skills, patience, and understanding of institutional processes. Every twist and turn became a stepping stone toward growth, and I now see that flexibility and resilience are just as important as ambition.
